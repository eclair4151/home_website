ParkPassport

Release Date: XXXX

ParkPassport is a mobile app made for users to easily find parks near them, and keep track of parks they have been to.
For this project I was contracted by the National Park Service to develop a native Android and iOS app, as well as a backend API and web admin dashboard. In addition to finding and tracking parks, the app allows you to add friends and see the parks they have been to, upload photos of the parks, log activities, and virtually visit certain national parks using google earth.
This service shows all official parks and landmarks recognized the National Park Service, as well as most local parks in the US providied by the free ArcGIS Park API. The ArcGIS API allows the app to download detailed gps park boundries for automatically detecting and logging park visits via the phones location.

National Park Service:
https://www.nps.gov

ArcGIS API:
https://developers.arcgis.com

ON/AORE Campus Challenge

Release Date: XXXXX

The Campus Challenge is a competition that happens once a year between many colleges and universities across the country, and the goal is to get as many people outside and active as possible.
Administration and students from the school sign up and endorse the competition, and students/faculty register under that school to participate. Each user is then tasked with uploading up to 5 photos a day of them doing activies to get points. Different activites are worth different amounts and the schools and users that ends up with the most points after 2 months gets rewarded with outdoor gear and sponsorship money for the schools outdoors club.
During the competitons, we got to run partnerships with many different prominent outdoor brands such as REI, Hydroflask, Northface, Adidas, etc, and had brand specific bonuses for extra points and giveaways.
Originally, this competition did not have a mobile app and the website was run by a third party. In my spare time I made a mobile app to allow drexel students to submit to the competition directly from thier phone (See Philly Activity Poster). After seeing my work, I was hired to write the official Android app and iOS from scratch for the competition, as well as rewrite the entire backend from scratch. Over the years I have help run the competition multiple times with hundreds of schools and thousands of users.
The competition was originally run by Outdoor Nation/Foundation (ON), and in 2019 was transfered to The Association of Outdoor Recration and Education (AORE)
In fall 2020 the competition was suspended and the apps/website were taken down because of COVID19 and has been on hold since.

Campus Challenge:
http://www.aorecampuschallenge.org/

AORE:
https://www.aore.org/

Outdoor Foundation:
https://outdoorindustry.org/participation/


MC Status Minecraft Widget

Release Date: XXXX

MC Status in a Mac and iPhone app i've written and open sourced in my spare time to solve a silly issue I had in my life. Normally when playing online games like Minecraft, in order to check if one of your friends is playing, you would have to start up your computer, open the game, and load up the server in order to see if anyone was online.
This solves that problem by allowing you to add servers you want to track in your app, and then you can place widgets on your homescreen, allowing you to quickly tell if anyone is online. This app was also written using Swift and Mac Catalyst which meant that the app is available for both iPhones and Mac. The app is used by ~1500 unique monthly active users and has 4.6 stars with over 400 ratings on the iPhone store globally.

Source Code:
https://github.com/eclair4151/MinecraftServerStatusWidget

YapTap Android App

Release Date: XXXX

YapTap was an app made by a company named 'Who Went Out Inc.', The company already had an iOS app, and I was contracted to write an Android app to pair with it.
The concept behind YapTap was mainly 'Snapchat for Audio'. It allowed you to record voice messages, or pick up to 30 seconds from a Spotify song and send it to other users with a message. iOS Jailbreak Tweaks

Release Date: XXXX

iPhones and the software that runs on them are generally thought to be pretty secure, but there is a well established group of people who are dedicated to jailbreaking and modifying iPhones. On jailbroken phones, there is a custom store added that allows you to download packages that change the way the OS runs that you normally dont have access to.
After being annoyed that i didnt have more control around certain aspects of how my phone worked, I became interested in creating custom open source jailbreak packages, and published them to the official jailbreak store, all of which now have thousands of downloads.

NotiBlock

(Pictured) NotiBlock is a tweak I made that adds a custom page in your phone settings app that allows you much more strict control over push notifications. It allows you to block or mute push notifications based on a combination of filters including: the app the sent it, the notification content, the notification time, and custom whitelists.
This lets me do things like automatically mute work emails on weekends, schedule specific times only certain people can reach me, and block notifications from apps which might send annoying notifications or ads. This tweak was also written about and praised by iDownloadBlog, a well known apple and jailbreak news website.

Search for Clubhouse

This is a tweak I wrote thats adds functionailty to official Clubhouse app. Clubhouse is an app that lets you join voice chats with people all over the world. Unfortunately though, the app drops you into a huge list of rooms with no way to filter them. This tweak injects into the app and adds a search bar to the top, allowing you to search for voice chats or events by specific topics and names.

Push For Apollo

Reddit is a website that has message boards and pages for specific topics for people to talk about. The official reddit app works ok, but many people prefer to use third party reddit clients. One downside of this though is that only the official reddit apps officially support push notifications because they dont provide an api for that. This tweak is one i made that allows you to install both the official reddit app, and a third party one. Then when the official reddit app gets a push notification, this tweak swaps some code around so it shows up as a notification from your third party app and opens up directly to it.

Drexel GPA Calculator

Release Date: XXXX

This app was the first app i ever wrote and was my entry into the mobile app world. Written for Android & iOS this app was made for Drexel students to help predict how thier GPA will be affected by certain grades. It integrated with Drexel One using HTML scraping to allow users to login and download thier class grades into the app automatically, and then search through the classes offered by Drexel and selec twhat grades they thought they would get. WiMap Hackathon Project

Release Date: XXXX

WiMap was a hackathon project I worked on with a group that took 1st place in 2017 in both the over all competition, and Lockheed Martins Best Complex Data Visualization Tool. This app was written in just 48 hours, and allows you to analyize your wifi by walking around your home and generating a wifi strength heat map. This lets you see where the wifi weak spots are in your house, and offers an option to analyize the heat map and suggest a new router placement that might get more even coverage.
