ParkPassport

Release Date: May 2019

ParkPassport is a mobile app made for users to easily find parks near them, and keep track of parks they have been to.
For this project I was contracted by the National Park Service to develop a native Android and iOS app, as well as a backend API and web admin dashboard. In addition to finding and tracking parks, the app allows you to add friends and see the parks they have been to, upload photos of the parks, log activities, and virtually visit certain national parks using google earth.
This service shows all official parks and landmarks recognized the National Park Service, as well as most local parks in the US provided by the free ArcGIS Park API. The ArcGIS API allows the app to download detailed gps park boundaries for automatically detecting and logging park visits via the phones location.

ON/AORE Campus Challenge

Release Date: August 2016

The Campus Challenge is a competition that happens once a year between many colleges and universities across the country, and the goal is to get as many people outside and active as possible.
Administration and students from the school sign up and endorse the competition, and students/faculty register under that school to participate. Each user is then tasked with uploading up to 5 photos a day of them doing activities to get points. Different activities are worth different amounts, and the schools and users that ends up with the most points after 2 months gets rewarded with outdoor gear and sponsorship money for the schools outdoors club.
During the competitions, we got to run partnerships with many prominent outdoor brands such as REI, Hydroflask, Northface, Adidas, etc, and had brand specific bonuses for extra points and giveaways.
Originally, this competition did not have a mobile app and the website was run by a third party. In my spare time, I made a mobile app to allow Drexel students to submit to the competition directly from their phone (See Philly Activity Poster). After seeing my work, I was hired to write the official Android app and iOS for the competition, as well as rewrite the entire backend from scratch. Over the years I have help run the competition multiple times with hundreds of schools and thousands of users.
The competition was originally run by Outdoor Nation/Foundation (ON), and in 2019 was transferred to The Association of Outdoor Recreation and Education (AORE)
In fall 2020 the competition was suspended and the apps/website were taken down because of COVID19, and has been on hold since.

MC Status Minecraft Widget

Release Date: July 2018

MC Status in a Mac and iPhone app i've written and open sourced in my spare time to solve a silly issue I had in my life. Normally when playing online games like Minecraft, in order to check if one of your friends is playing, you would have to start up your computer, open the game, and load up the server in order to see if anyone was online.

The app is used by ~1500 unique monthly active users and has 4.6 stars with over 400 ratings on the iPhone store globally.

iOS Jailbreak Tweaks

Release Dates: August 2018 - 2021

iPhones and the software that runs on them are generally thought to be pretty secure, but there is a well established group of people who are dedicated to jailbreaking and modifying iPhones. On jailbroken phones, there is a custom store added that allows you to download packages that change the way the OS runs that you normally don't have access to.
After being annoyed that I didn't have more control around certain aspects of how my phone worked, I became interested in creating custom open source jailbreak packages, and published them to the official jailbreak store, all of which now have thousands of downloads.

NotiBlock

(Pictured) NotiBlock is a tweak I made that adds a custom page in your phone settings app that allows you much more strict control over push notifications. It allows you to block or mute push notifications based on a combination of filters, including: the app the sent it, the notification content, the notification time, and custom whitelists.
This lets me do things like automatically mute work emails on weekends, schedule specific times only certain people can reach me, and block notifications from apps which might send annoying notifications or ads. This tweak was also written about and praised by iDownloadBlog, a well known apple and jailbreak news website.

Push For Apollo

Reddit is a website that has message boards and pages for specific topics for people to talk about. The official Reddit app works ok, but many people prefer to use third party Reddit clients. One downside of this though is that only the official Reddit apps officially support push notifications because they don't provide an API for that. This tweak is one I made that allows you to install both the official Reddit app, and a third party one. Then when the official Reddit app gets a push notification, this tweak swaps some code around, so it shows up as a notification from your third party app and opens up directly to it.

Multi-Sensored QuadFusion Hackathon Project

Release Date: January 2015

Multi-Sensored QuadFusion was a hackathon project I worked on that took first place at DragonHacks 2015. I worked with a group creating an interface that allows anyone to control a drone just by looking around and pointing. The project used a Google Glass and its built-in camera to run OpenCV and detect bright stickers on the drone. It then sent commands to the drone using an Intel Edison board over WiFi to instruct the drone to move to the user's center of view. Additionally, the system connected to a Myo armband over Bluetooth, and allowed you to make the drone fly closer or farther from you based on your arm position.

Flight Simulator Hardware

Release Date: June 2020

In addition to making 3D models for flight simulators, I like to make custom hardware interfaces too, which allow me to control the flight sim with physical hardware. Most of the hardware panels you find online is extremely expensive though, costing hundreds or thousands of dollars. This was the main reason I decided to see if I could enhance my flight simulator experience with some homemade tech. For this project, I used an Arduino, which detects the user input and controls the LED display inside a custom designed 3D printed housing. It then communicates over USB with a custom flight sim plugin I made, which lets you control the planes in the sim via the physical panels.

Reverse Engineering Drexel One's API

Release Date: December 2016

Drexel University offers an official mobile app to their students which uses a private API that fetches data like grades, class schedule, meal plan, etc. Unfortunately, they don't make it available to students, so I spent some time reverse engineering it and seeing how it worked so I could use it for some personal projects. I used Charles Proxy with a custom TLS root certificate on my phone to run a MITM attack on the app and inspect the traffic, and then decompiled the Android app to reverse engineer how they generated the unique request authentication hash.
